Intro

Water pollution is when substances build up in the water to a point where it causes problems for people and animals. There are two main types of water pollution: nonpoint-source pollution and point-source pollution. If pollution comes from one source, then it is point-source pollution. If it comes from multiple sources, then it is nonpoint-source pollution.

Sometimes it’s obvious when water has been polluted, like an oil spill. But most of the time, it’s not. There are two main ways you can measure the quality of water. One way is to test the chemical indicators, or the concentration of different chemicals in the water. The other way is to test the biological indicators, or see what kind of life the water can support. If the river can’t support any life, then the quality of the river must be poor.

The problem

Atmospheric deposition is one of the many causes of water pollution. It is when chemicals released into the air enter the atmosphere and fall back to the earth as rain. Water pollution has so many causes and it’s why it is such a difficult problem to solve.

Sewage is another cause. [stats]

When so much sewage is released into the environment, the nutrients from it can cause the growth of harmful algal blooms. These blooms remove the oxygen from the water and kill the life present in the water.

However, pollution is not strictly limited to chemicals. It can be biological as well. The introduction of invasive species into water ecosystems can drastically change the environment. Because they have no natural predators outside their normal habitat, they can run rampant and crowd out native life.

How can you stop this?

  • Educate yourself. This website is a great first step on educating yourself on water pollution.
  • You can reuse, reduce, recycle
  • Use reusable plastic products
  • Use less water
  • Take showers, not baths
  • Turn off the water when brushing your teeth
  • Fix leaks
  • Recycle any recyclable products
  • Clean up garbage in your neighborhood because it can end up in a water source
  • Properly dispose of hazardous materials
  • Tells others about the cause
  • Donate to reputable charities that help with water conservation efforts
  • Find activities that will help with the cause
  • Demand clean water
  • Be mindful of what you eat
  • Eat sustainable seafood (“line caught”, “diver caught”, “sustainably caught”, “sustainably harvested”)
